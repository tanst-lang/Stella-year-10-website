Tips and Tricks

  • If you need to count how many blocks there are in a line, rather than counting each block follow these simple steps.
    1. turn on your coordinates.
    2. go to one end of your line of blocks and take note of your coordinates.
    3. travel to the other end of your line of blocks and note your new coordinates
    4. the difference in your coordinates are how many blocks there are in the line.
  • If you have an animal farm and are sick of the animals escaping when you open the gate, simply put carpet ontop of one or more of the fence blocks. You will be able to jump ontop of it to get in and out but mobs can't
  • rather than making chests to store your items try using barrels instead. Chests require 8 wooden planks to craft while barrels only require 6 wooden planks and two wooden slabs. This means that for the same amount of wood that creates 7 chests you can make 8 barrels. chests also cannot open if another block is ontop of it (exept for a few exeptions) but barrels can. Barrels also provide a more discrete look as you could have a floor made of barrels and no one would bat an eye whereas chests are much more obvious.
  • if you are strip mining and gravel starts falling infront of you, break the bottom one and quickly place a torch in its place. You will have to do this quickly, but if done correctly the gravel will fall onto the torch and break, creating an almost waterfall like effect
  • use old wooden tools as fuel for your furnace. That's right you can use old wooden tools like the wooden pickaxe in the depths of your chest as emergency fuel for your furnace, just pop it in where your regular fuel would go.
  • on the topic of furnaces did you know you can use a campfire to cook your food instead? simply get your campfire and place the desired food on it, you can even cook up to four items at a time!
